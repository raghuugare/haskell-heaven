I started this to share some of my little nuggets of learning on the way to Haskell heaven. Needless to say, there are tons of various pieces of useful info about Haskell, its awesome features, community & eco-system on the internet. But what I have felt lacking among students intending to learn Functional Programming is a deep appreciation & understanding of the core concepts on top of which the whole realm of Functional Programming (of which Haskell is a torch-bearer!) is built, namely the concept of functions, types, and how they are beautifully represented by Haskell in their purest forms. Knowing these concepts properly leads one to experience Aha! moments; after all, programming ought to be filled with such Joys & Thrills of discovery!

Anyways, this series of articles is NOT a Haskell Tutorial. Interested readers can consult the References page for pointers regarding these. What I want to do here is try & share the subtle patterns & beauty in Haskell in particular, especially highlighting how they reflect the core mathematical ideas. If these "essays" could stoke the fire in you & your friends to plunge into the Pilgrimage to Haskell Heaven, I would be so happy indeed! And I'm sure that any such traveller will be rewarded with unforgettable "views" along the way!

Happy Journey! And Let's Get Started !!!

Hello World

How about the "typical" program that prints "Hello World"?
Well, here goes the code of such a program called Hello.hs: 

				
						-- Hello.hs
						main :: IO ()
						main = print "Hello World"

Note: Do not let the program-snippet syntax fool you about the I/O system of Haskell being "typical" in any way--especially if you are from an imperative programming language like Java, C++, etc.

Haskell is one of the rare languages with an almost maniacal obsession about the concept of purity. In a programming language, being pure means that all side-effects are modelled explicitly, rather than being present in an invisible, unpredictable way.

Please don't worry. We have a lot to speak about the features of Haskell in this space.
